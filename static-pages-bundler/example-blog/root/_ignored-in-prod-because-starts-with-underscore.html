Reverse Drafting
User Interfaces

by Eric Fortis August 19, 2021

1. The Mindset

As reverse drafting is for improving existing apps, some people think of it as finding problems on their work. Therefore, they understandably feel somehow uncomfortable.

On the other hand, some feel positively when discovering improvements for their apps. Here are three reasons why:

  1. Once you've decided to build a good app, finding usability issues tells you that you're moving forward.
  2. As no one is omniscient, feeling bad about it (as long as it's an honest mistake) it's fighting nature.
  3. It's easier and faster, you won't need to make up excuses, and remember lies.

Of course there's people who are indifferent about how they feel about problems in their app. But whether you are neutral or negative, I hope the points above help you find some joy when reverse drafting your app.

2. When?

In order to reverse draft, you have to wait until you don't have the app highly memorized. Also, make sure your mind is at 100%. That is, do it with a fresh mind, after a nap, or waking up.

3. Pick a part of the app

Ideally, start with a small part, let's say 5-6 units. By units, I mean fields (input or output), instructions, or the options in of a drop down.

4. Start

List the UI elements, not as they are, but as text. For example, don't make distinctions between Buttons, Checkboxes, or other kind of elements.

Stop right when you start feeling that you are making too much effort to remember. For example, don't force your visual memory.
